What is Pomodoro and How it works?

What is Pomodoro

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management system that encourages people to work with time. This technology was developed in the late 1980s by then-university student Francesco Cirillo. He used this technique when he was in college to keep his mind focused on his studies. "Pomodoro" is Italian for tomatoes. Cirillo used a timer in his kitchen that had the shape of a tomato.

In this technique, After work on the task for 25 minutes, you need to take a short five-minute break. After four of these Pomodoros which means, four repetitions can take a 15 to 30 minutes break.

This technique will teach you to work with time instead of struggling against time.

How it works?

01-STEP

CHOOSE A TASK

Something big, something small, something you’ve postponed for millions of years it doesn’t matter. What matters is that it is something that needs your full, indelible attention.

02-STEP

START THE TIMER AND WORK

Start the timer and do the work for 25 minutes. Stick to the task you have chosen for the entire session, and if you suddenly realize that you have something else to do, write it down on a piece of paper.

03-STEP

TAKE A SHORT BREAK

After spending Pomodoro without interruption get 5 minutes break. You can click the Play button to listen to the non-word music. Try to refresh your mind. Grab a cup of coffee. Avoid work-related things and try to relax your mind.

04-STEP

EVERY 4 POMODOROS, TAKE A LONGER BREAK

Once you have completed four Pomodoros, you can take a long break of 15 to 30 minutes. Your brain uses this time to absorb new dangerrmation and to relax before the next round of Pomodoros.
